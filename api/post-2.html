Experts Tested Octopus DNA and Discovered It Isn’t From Our Planet

A group of experts decided to decipher the DNA code of cephalopods and see what came out of it. One of the main reasons for this was their ability to swiftly change neural properties that impact memory and learning capability.

The skin, suckers, and nervous system are all complex biological structures that are encoded by numerous cephalopod-specific genes.

The brain networks are composed of half a billion neurons, the bulk of which are disseminated out of the skull without the need for a spinal cord, as in vertebrates.

One of the most astonishing aspects of these animals' brain systems is that they can perform cognitive functions even if one of their limbs is destroyed. This is a fantastic example of cutting-edge biological engineering at work.

This is due to reflectins, which are six protein genes that govern how light bounces off the octopus' skin.

Octopuses are without a doubt one of science's greatest mysteries. Because of their enormous intricacy, many specialists believe they are extraterrestrials or have alien genetic baggage.
